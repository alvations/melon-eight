# Aesthetic review, second pass: does the craft hold at beta-1

The craft-only reviewer again: prose, mood, visual and sonic identity, and
whether the arcs tell a story or hold a vibe. `docs/REVIEW_AESTHETIC.md` (8/10) is
the record. Since then the changes in my lane are two: the localization was
finished to the last string (which, for a game that presents itself in eight
languages, is an *aesthetic* fact, not just an engineering one), and the per-arc
soundscapes were verified to actually play. So this is a short pass on whether the
mood survives all the way to the edges.

**Verdict: 8/10, held.** The craft is intact and, in one specific way, more
complete than last time: a player in Japanese or Vietnamese now meets the same
finished surface an English player does, with no stray English word breaking the
spell mid-sentence. That matters more here than in most games, because the mood is
the product and a single untranslated "furthest" in the corner is a crack in it.
The score holds rather than rises because the two craft notes from last time, the
too-explicit coach line and the mood-versus-story question, are untouched.

## What improved for the mood, specifically

- **The spell now holds in every language.** The last English leaks (the HUD
  marker, the hesitation lines, the ending button, the control labels for screen
  readers) are gone. For an atmosphere-first game, an untranslated fragment is not
  a bug ticket, it is a broken immersion, and closing them is a real aesthetic
  win, not a chore. The eight-language identity is now honest to the corner.
- **The hesitation lines are good writing and now everyone gets them.** "Doubt
  stops you. Read it once more." is exactly the register this game does best, and
  it was previously English-only. Reaching for it in every locale is the right
  kind of care.
- **The soundscapes are confirmed present, not theoretical.** I was skeptical last
  time that the per-arc room tone was audible; it is there, deliberately quiet,
  and now guarded so it cannot silently vanish. The restraint (room tone, not
  music) remains the correct call even if it costs the arcs an obvious "theme."

## What is unchanged, and I still want fixed

### 1. The coach utterance still tells where the game implies.
My single sharpest note from last time, untouched. The passenger's arming line is
still the most expository sentence in the whole game, and it is now translated
faithfully into eight languages, which means the *one* over-explicit moment is now
over-explicit in eight languages. Faithful translation of a line I would cut is
not progress on the line. Rewrite it quieter, then translate that.

### 2. Tone is still doing the work "story" should.
The flashbacks remain lovely fragments that gesture at a backstory without
resolving into one. Nothing this pass changed that, and nothing needed to; I
restate it only because at a beta gate it is worth being honest that this is a
game with a superb *mood* and a barely-there *plot*, and the marketing voice
should keep promising the first.

### 3. The frame keeps growing around the same picture.
A settings panel that now also remembers your reading register and difficulty in
the save is more chrome, tasteful chrome, around the same short, silent middle.
Every pass I note the ratio; every pass it tips a little further toward the menu.
Guard the silence of the opening; it is still the best thing you have made.

## Gate verdict

From a craft standpoint this is a clean beta-1. The prose is still the best part,
the identity is still coherent, the restraint still holds, and now it holds in
every language it claims to speak. Ship it. Then cut the one loud line, and decide
whether the tone gets a story or stays, proudly, a tone.

## One line

> The mood now survives all the way into the eighth language, which for this game
> is the whole point, and it is intact. Cut the one sentence that explains itself,
> keep the opening as silent as it is, and give this lovely tone either a story or
> the confidence to stay a tone.
