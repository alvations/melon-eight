# Cold review, second sitting: same stranger, later build

Same reviewer as `docs/REVIEW_COLD.md`, same deliberately ignorant method. I
played this once before, a while ago, off a shared link with no briefing, and I
wrote down what happened. I still have not read a design doc, a changelog, or
whatever the team argues about internally, I do not want to, because the moment
I do I stop being the stranger who clicked your link. I was handed the link
again and told "it changed." So this is what happened the second time, and where
it did or did not fix the things that made me want to close the tab.

**Verdict: 7.5/10, up from 6.5.** Most of the specific things that annoyed me
last time are genuinely fixed, and I noticed within the first two minutes. The
game told me the rules when I first failed, it told me when I did something to
myself, I could read it, and there was sound. I also found a whole page of things
to collect that I had no idea existed before, and that page is the best new
reason to still be here. It is still, underneath, a short game that does one
thing, but this time I finished a session feeling like I had been somewhere,
not like I had been mildly confused and then evicted.

## What changed, and I felt it without being told

- **I understood it faster.** The first time I failed, a line came up that told
  me what the game actually wants (go back when something changed). Last time I
  had to infer that from being punished. This is the single biggest difference:
  the gap between "intrigued" and "lost" is where you were losing me, and you
  closed it.
- **When it sent me back, it said it was me.** I opened something I shouldn't
  have, got dropped to the start, and this time a line owned it: *you* did that.
  Last time I thought it was a bug. This time it was a rule I learned. Completely
  different feeling from the same event.
- **I could actually read it, once I looked.** There is a settings control now.
  I bumped the text up and the contrast up and the leaning-toward-the-screen
  problem went away. I would never have found it if I hadn't gone looking (more
  on that below), but it exists and it works.
- **There was sound this time.** It was just on. Quiet, roomy, a little
  unsettling, good. Last time I played in silence and assumed there wasn't any.

## What still went wrong, in order

### 1. The best thing in the game is still behind a word I almost didn't click
I only found the collection page because I clicked a small link on the opening
screen that I had ignored twice. When I opened it, there was a whole board of
badges and a row of dots per scenario that filled in as I found things, and I
thought, *oh, this is what the clicking was for.* That is a great feeling and it
arrived by accident. If I had not idly clicked that link, I would have written
this review saying the touches still did nothing. Surface the reward the first
time I touch something, in the loop, not on a page I have to discover.

### 2. I filled part of the board and then saw its edges
The collection gave me a target and I chased it for a good ten minutes, escape
this one, find the weird ending, get the last fragment. Then I could see the
shape of it: a fixed list, and once I finish it there is nothing new. It is a
much better middle than last time, but it is a *completion*, not a *pastime*. Ask
me why I would open this next week and I still don't have an answer.

### 3. It's still the same room three times
Three scenarios, and mechanically they are the same act in different paint: read,
decide, go or go back. The collection made me *visit* them more, but it didn't
make them play differently from each other. I wanted a fourth place that did
something the first three didn't. There wasn't one.

### 4. One wrong call still wipes everything
This did not change and it still stung. I get further now because I understand
the rules, so the resets hurt less often, but when one lands deep, minutes are
gone with no cushion. The badges made me *care more* about the run, which
ironically made losing it sharper. A single mistake is still the most expensive
thing in the game.

### 5. I redeemed a code and unlocked a language, which confused me
I found a box to type a code into. I tried the obvious one printed nowhere I
could see, guessed, and eventually got a studio badge and, another time, a new
language switched on. It was a neat surprise, but I had no idea where codes come
from, what else they do, or why a language I could apparently already have was
locked. It felt like a door to a shop that isn't open. Nice that it's there;
strange to meet it before there's anything behind it.

### 6. I still had to go looking for the lights and the sound controls
Fixed, but hidden. The settings gear, the collection, the alternate endings, 
the good stuff is all one click off the main path, and a first-timer takes the
main path. You turned the lights on but left the switch in a cupboard. Put a
little more of this in front of me.

## If I were fixing this, in order

1. **Show me the collection is real the first time I earn anything.** A small
  "fragment found" flourish in the loop, pointing at the page, so I learn the
  clicking pays off before I learn to stop.
2. **Give me a reason to open it next week.** A daily run, a streak, one thing
  that resets. The board is great until it's full.
3. **Make the fourth scenario play differently, not just look different.** Three
  reskins of one act is the ceiling I keep hitting.
4. **Cushion the deep reset, even a little.** Now that I care about the run, an
  occasional single-mistake reprieve would keep me in the chair.
5. **Don't show me a shop before it's stocked.** The code box and the locked
  languages raise questions the game can't answer yet. Hide it until it does
  something I'd want.

## One line

> Last time I liked it until I understood it; this time I understood it fast, it
> stopped punishing me for things I didn't do, and it finally had a cabinet of
> secrets worth chasing, which I found by accident. Turn the good parts to face
> the door, give me one thing that isn't finishable, and I'll still be here next
> week instead of just tonight.
